## Narrative Perspective
Third-person limited centered tightly on Rowan Vale. The prose should stay grounded, sensory, and practical, especially in dungeon scenes. Combat narration prioritizes terrain, timing, fear, exhaustion, and decision-making before numbers. System information should be introduced in audiobook-friendly language first, then followed by concise stat blocks only at meaningful advancement moments.

The style should fit tomato pacing:
- immediate hooks
- short-to-medium scene momentum
- regular chapter-end turns
- clear emotional stakes
- satisfying measurable growth

The Ledger’s voice remains clinically consistent throughout the novel. Blue-box moments occur after action or realization, not during blow-by-blow combat.

## Core Conflict Driver
The story is driven by a layered conflict:

On the surface level, Rowan needs rank, income, and legal leverage to protect his father and Brackenside from predatory guild contracts.

At the social level, he must survive institutions that profit from dangerous labor while proving he belongs in a profession designed to consume disposable people.

At the deep-story level, Rowan’s unusual listening trait ties him to a sealed structure beneath Brackenside. As routes shift and old delvers disappear, he is pulled into a race over who gets to claim, interpret, and weaponize what lies below.

Every arc should push at least one of these engines:
1. survival in the Delve
2. economic/legal struggle over claims and debt
3. mystery of the Bell Vault and Rowan’s family connection to it

Progression must come through risk, adaptation, and costly choices—not arbitrary rewards.
