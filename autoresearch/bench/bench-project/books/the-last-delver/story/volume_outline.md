## Volume Overview
This 40-chapter novel is structured as **4 arcs of 10 chapters each**, functioning like four compact volumes inside one book. This fits tomato pacing: fast hook, steady gains, clear cliffhangers, and escalating reveals.

---

## Volume 1: Unproven Below
**Chapter Range:** 1-10  
**Core Conflict:** Rowan is forced into the Delve without rank or protection after a rescue crisis reveals his unusual perception and draws guild attention.  
**Key Turning Points:**
- Ch1: Emergency collapse under Lift Tower Nine; Rowan enters danger immediately.
- Ch2: His echo-based perception identifies a survivable route and wins the first real Ledger recognition.
- Ch3: Rowan sets a concrete short-term goal: earn legal provisional delver status before his family debt is called in.
- Ch4-5: First independent low-route runs in the Hollow Veins; he learns basic combat is uglier and slower than stories.
- Ch6: Tessa Quill flags irregular salvage records tied to Rowan’s rescue.
- Ch7: Rowan’s first meaningful team-up with Lin Vey goes badly but proves his route-reading value.
- Ch8: Joren Pike tries to seize an unregistered find.
- Ch9: Rowan survives a swarm event and secures enough proof for provisional standing.
- Ch10: He receives a path-leaning skill evolution opportunity tied to listening, mapping, and hazard reading.

**Payoff Goal:** Rowan ceases to be prey-level labor and becomes a legally recognized novice delver with a dangerous but credible edge.

### Golden First Three Chapters Rule
- **Chapter 1:** A lower support line snaps during a rescue run; Rowan is trapped below with workers, monsters, and collapsing stone.
- **Chapter 2:** Rowan’s Residual Hearing lets him detect a void behind a false wall and guide survivors through an unstable bypass, earning the Ledger’s first formal notice.
- **Chapter 3:** After guild officials move to bury the incident, Rowan realizes legal rank is the only shield he can get; his first concrete goal becomes clear: pass provisional delver qualification and claim enough salvage to protect his household.

## Volume 2: Claims in the Dark
**Chapter Range:** 11-20  
**Core Conflict:** Rowan begins competing for routes, rights, and survival as the guild tries to either control or discredit him.  
**Key Turning Points:**
- Ch11: First official novice descent under observation.
- Ch12: Rowan unlocks a utility skill through near-failure rather than combat triumph.
- Ch13-14: He and Lin contest an unstable route with Brass Lantern contract crews.
- Ch15: Tessa uncovers layered debt manipulation affecting Brackenside families.
- Ch16: A profitable salvage chamber turns into a legal ambush over claim markers.
- Ch17: Rowan’s father reveals partial truth about an old sealed route.
- Ch18: Rowan suffers his first real defeat against a stronger human opponent and learns the gap between cunning and rank.
- Ch19: Naia identifies an abnormal spore mutation linked to deeper disturbances.
- Ch20: Rowan secures a disputed claim through evidence, not force alone, embarrassing Joren publicly.

**Payoff Goal:** Rowan establishes himself as a legitimate factor in Brackenside’s power balance and gains a small coalition of allies.

---

## Volume 3: The Silt Galleries
**Chapter Range:** 21-30  
**Core Conflict:** Rowan leads or co-leads a hazardous expedition into shifting mid-depth sectors where ordinary maps fail and the deep begins reacting to him.  
**Key Turning Points:**
- Ch21: A sudden route collapse cuts off several teams; a race to a safer gallery begins.
- Ch22: Rowan’s listening ability behaves differently in flooded stone.
- Ch23: First major mid-book stat punctuation after a severe hazard clearance.
- Ch24: Brother Cal reveals the term “resonance chamber” and hints Rowan’s mother knew of one.
- Ch25-26: Team dynamics strain under fear, oxygen scarcity, and conflicting priorities.
- Ch27: Rowan earns an evolved path feature tied to route memory or vibration analysis.
- Ch28: They encounter evidence that monsters are being displaced upward from lower strata.
- Ch29: A mini-boss or environmental boss encounter in the flooded galleries forces hard triage.
- Ch30: The expedition returns with proof that Brackenside sits above a sealed deep structure of major value.

**Payoff Goal:** Rowan moves from reactive scavenger to emerging expedition anchor. The scale of the mystery expands beyond local debt and guild corruption.

---

## Volume 4: The Bell Vault
**Chapter Range:** 31-40  
**Core Conflict:** Multiple factions converge on the sealed deep structure under Brackenside, forcing Rowan to choose what to reveal, what to claim, and what to destroy.  
**Key Turning Points:**
- Ch31: Brass Lantern brings in stronger personnel and legal pressure.
- Ch32: Moss Knives steal or leak route information, accelerating the race.
- Ch33: Rowan and Elias finally confront the truth about Rowan’s mother’s last expedition.
- Ch34: Tessa risks her position to formalize Rowan’s claim rights.
- Ch35: Serik Dane corners Rowan underground; Rowan survives through terrain, not superior force.
- Ch36: Entry into the Bell Vault perimeter.
- Ch37: The Vault responds to Rowan’s listening trait and offers partial system-recognized inheritance.
- Ch38: Joren’s greed triggers a catastrophic reaction or breach.
- Ch39: Rowan chooses to save survivors and preserve evidence rather than seize immediate maximum power.
- Ch40: The Bell Vault is not fully opened, but Rowan secures a rank breakthrough, Brackenside gains a temporary reprieve, and a deeper call confirms the series-scale conflict.

**Payoff Goal:** Deliver a satisfying book-ending win—Rowan protects his town and earns real advancement—while opening a larger series mystery about the Delves’ true purpose.

## Chapter-by-Chapter Macro Pacing Notes

### Chapters 1-10
- Frequent gains, visible improvement
- Tight stakes: debt, legality, survival
- Small stat sheets only at major milestones: Ch2, Ch5/6, Ch10

### Chapters 11-20
- Fewer level-style surges, more skill refinement
- Introduce legal and social consequences
- Mid-volume setback is essential to avoid effortless rise

### Chapters 21-30
- Harder gains tied to exploration and hazard mastery
- Focus on team stress, environment, and puzzle-combat
- One major progression beat around Ch23-27

### Chapters 31-40
- Advancement is rare and climactic
- Emotional payoffs, family secrets, faction collision
- Final stat punctuation only after decisive book-end milestone
